## Save Files

### Where are the save files?

- **Windows:** `C:\Users\[user]\AppData\LocalLow\WASEKU\Data Center\saves`
- **Windows (environment):** `%homepath%\AppData\LocalLow\WASEKU\Data Center\saves`
- **Linux:** `~/.local/share/Steam/steamapps/compatdata/4170200/pfx/drive_c/users/steamuser/AppData/LocalLow/Waseku/Data Center/saves/`

## Hardware and EOL

### What does End of Life (EOL) mean?

In real life, EOL means a manufacturer stops making and supporting a server model, including updates and technical help. Using EOL hardware poses security risks and compliance issues. Organizations should plan to replace or upgrade hardware before reaching EOL to avoid unexpected problems and costs.

### What does EOL mean in-game?

The **EOL timer** indicates how long your hardware is safe. When the timer runs out, the server displays a yellow warning mark, meaning it could break from that point on. When a device breaks, it shows a red error mark and stops working. You need to replace it yourself or send a technician to do it for you; in that case, you do not have to re-cable the device.

### Is it normal for devices to break before EOL?

Yes, sometimes. If a server has 0 effective operations per second before EOL, it can still be a broken device. A broken server is off and no canvas is shown on it.

### What if a server shows 0 effective operations per second?

If the IP is correct and the device is cabled properly, the issue can still depend on wrong IP settings, wrong cabling, or in some cases pathing issues. Turning the device off and on again or recabling it might help.

## Support and Bug Reporting

### How do I report a bug to Waseku?

Please post in the official bug-report channel.

To help Waseku find the issue faster, include:

- A description of what triggered the issue.
- A screenshot of the problem or your layout.

### How can I support the dev?

The best way to support **Waseku** is by being active in the community, reporting bugs clearly, and being patient while he works through the technical side of the game.

### How do channel links work?

Use the channel-number format:

- [Discord support channel](https://discord.gg/waseku-{channelid})

Replace `{channelid}` with the channel number you want to open directly.
